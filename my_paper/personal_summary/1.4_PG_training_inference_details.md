# Training Inference Details

## Intuition

Executing SBI means generating parameter-data pairs, training the selected neural estimator, and then using it to access the posterior for $x_o$ (Guide, p10:L43-L50; p11:L1-L5).

Simulations are generated by sampling parameters from the prior and running the simulator; because simulation and training are decoupled, simulated datasets can be produced offline, parallelized, and reused across models (Guide, p11:L3-L12).

Reliable training requires train/validation/test splits, monitoring training and validation losses, checkpointing, early stopping, and systematic tracking of architecture and hyperparameter choices (Guide, p11:L14-L39).

The necessary simulation budget has no universal formula; it depends on simulator complexity, data dimension, parameter dimension, architecture size, and model complexity (Guide, p12:L17-L31; p21:L6-L13).

For NPE, inference is fast because posterior samples come directly from the trained network; for NLE/NRE, inference needs MCMC or VI on top of the trained network, which adds runtime and additional failure modes (Guide, p12:L1-L15; p30:L27-L37).

## Mathematical Framework

The simulation dataset is

$$
\mathcal{D}_N=\{(\theta_i,x_i)\}_{i=1}^{N},
\qquad
\theta_i\sim p(\theta),\quad x_i\sim p_{\text{sim}}(x\mid\theta_i).
$$

This is the shared training data structure for the main SBI algorithms (Guide, p11:L3-L12).

For NPE, the training objective is

$$
\mathcal{L}(\phi)
=
\mathbb{E}_{(\theta,x)\sim p(\theta,x)}
\left[-\log q_\phi(\theta\mid x)\right],
$$

which encourages the network to assign high probability to the parameters that generated each simulated datum (Guide, p4:L29-L40).

For amortized inference, the trained model is reused:

$$
x_o \mapsto q_\phi(\theta\mid x_o),
$$

without new simulations or retraining (Guide, p12:L1-L3).

For NLE or NRE, the trained network becomes a surrogate inside a posterior sampler:

$$
\theta^{(m)} \sim \tilde{p}_\phi(\theta\mid x_o),
$$

where $\tilde{p}_\phi$ is accessed by MCMC or VI rather than direct neural sampling (Guide, p12:L4-L15).

Validation loss is also a hyperparameter objective: for NPE, negative log-probability under the estimated posterior is principled because it upper-bounds the KL divergence between true and approximate posterior (Guide, p32:L33-L42).
