# Choice Of Network Details

## Intuition

The main SBI families differ by what the neural network learns: NPE learns the posterior $q_\phi(\theta\mid x)$, NLE learns the likelihood $q_\phi(x\mid\theta)$, and NRE learns a likelihood-to-evidence ratio through classification (Guide, p8:L36-L44; p9:L9-L37).

NPE is attractive when many observations must be analyzed, because after training it can return posterior samples in milliseconds with a direct forward pass (Guide, p9:L1-L8; p30:L27-L39).

NLE and NRE are attractive for datasets of many i.i.d. observations because likelihoods can be multiplied or log-likelihoods summed across trials, allowing networks trained on single observations to handle variable-size datasets (Guide, p9:L14-L28; p31:L41-L49).

For high-dimensional raw observations, NPE and NRE are usually more natural because they take $x$ as input and can use embedding networks end-to-end; NLE is better suited to low-dimensional data or validated summaries (Guide, p31:L15-L29).

Normalizing flows are a common NPE network because they model non-Gaussian and multimodal posteriors while supporting sampling and density evaluation (Guide, p10:L1-L12).

Diffusion and flow-matching models offer more flexibility for complex or high-dimensional posteriors, but sampling or density evaluation is typically more expensive (Guide, p10:L13-L18).

## Mathematical Framework

NPE trains a conditional posterior estimator

$$
q_\phi(\theta\mid x)\approx p(\theta\mid x),
$$

and uses direct posterior sampling at inference time (Guide, p8:L39-L44; p9:L1-L8).

NLE trains a conditional likelihood estimator

$$
q_\phi(x\mid\theta)\approx p(x\mid\theta),
$$

then combines it with the prior during inference:

$$
p(\theta\mid x_o)\propto q_\phi(x_o\mid\theta)p(\theta),
$$

usually sampled with MCMC or VI (Guide, p9:L9-L13; p12:L4-L15).

For i.i.d. observations $x_{1:N}$, NLE uses

$$
p(\theta\mid x_{1:N}) \propto
\left[\prod_{i=1}^{N} q_\phi(x_i\mid\theta)\right]p(\theta),
$$

which explains why one single-trial likelihood network can handle variable numbers of trials (Guide, p9:L14-L28; p31:L41-L49).

NRE learns a classifier-based ratio

$$
r_\phi(x,\theta)\approx \frac{p(x\mid\theta)}{p(x)},
$$

by distinguishing joint samples $(x,\theta)\sim p(x,\theta)$ from independent marginal samples $(x,\theta)\sim p(x)p(\theta)$ (Guide, p9:L29-L37).
