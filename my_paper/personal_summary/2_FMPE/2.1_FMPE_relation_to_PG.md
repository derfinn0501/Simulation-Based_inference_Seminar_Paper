# FMPE Relation To The Practical Guide

## Intuition

The practical guide describes the general SBI workflow: define simulator and prior, choose data representation and inference network, generate simulations, train, validate, and analyze the posterior (PG, p5:L27-L36). FMPE changes mainly the **choice of inference network**, not the overall workflow.

In the guide's terminology, FMPE belongs to neural posterior estimation: it directly approximates $p(\theta\mid x)$ with $q_\phi(\theta\mid x)$ (PG, p8:L36-L44; FMPE, p4:L35-L43).

What is new is the density estimator. The practical guide lists normalizing flows as common NPE networks and notes that diffusion and flow-matching models offer more flexibility for complex posteriors, usually at higher sampling or density-evaluation cost (PG, p10:L1-L18).

FMPE makes this concrete: instead of a discrete normalizing flow with specialized invertible layers, it uses a continuous normalizing flow parameterized by an unconstrained vector field. This allows larger and more flexible architectures, especially when $x$ is high-dimensional (FMPE, p1:L29-L37; FMPE, p2:L80-L85; FMPE, p6:L29-L39).

So, relative to the practical guide, FMPE is best understood as an advanced posterior-network choice inside the NPE branch:

$$
\text{SBI workflow}
\rightarrow
\text{NPE}
\rightarrow
\text{FMPE as posterior density estimator}.
$$

## Mathematical Framework

The practical guide's NPE objective is

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\text{NPE}}
=
-
\mathbb{E}_{(\theta,x)\sim p(\theta,x)}
\left[
\log q_\phi(\theta\mid x)
\right],
$$

where

$$
p(\theta,x)=p(\theta)p(x\mid\theta).
$$

This trains a conditional density estimator directly on prior-simulator samples (PG, p4:L29-L40; FMPE, p4:L35-L43).

FMPE keeps the same target posterior but changes the training objective:

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\text{FMPE}}
=
\mathbb{E}
\left[
\left\|
v_{t,x}(\theta_t)
-
u_t(\theta_t\mid\theta_1)
\right\|^2
\right].
$$

The replacement is therefore:

$$
\text{maximize posterior log density}
\quad
\longrightarrow
\quad
\text{regress a posterior-generating vector field}.
$$

The simulator side stays the same:

$$
\theta_1 \sim p(\theta),
\qquad
x \sim p(x\mid\theta_1).
$$

This is why FMPE fits cleanly into the practical-guide SBI workflow: it does not require likelihood evaluations, gradients of the simulator, or changes to how the simulated training data are generated (PG, p2:L21-L29; PG, p6:L2-L12; FMPE, p5:L10-L22).

The main conceptual update is that the posterior is represented by a flow field

$$
v_{t,x}:\Theta\times[0,1]\times\mathcal{X}\rightarrow\Theta,
$$

rather than only by an explicit sequence of discrete invertible transformations. This vector field transports a base distribution into the posterior for the given observation $x$ (FMPE, p3:L47-L53; FMPE, p4:L1-L10).
